# Business-Kpi-Dashboard
This project presents an end-to-end Business Intelligence solution designed to monitor company performance through an executive-level dashboard. The goal is to simulate a real-world scenario where business leaders track key performance indicators (KPIs), identify trends, and make data-driven decisions.

**Objectives**

Track overall business performance, Analyze revenue and profit trends, Identify top-performing products, regions, and sales channels, Deliver actionable, data-driven business insights

**Tools & Technologies**

**SQL Server** – Data cleaning, transformation, and KPI preparation
**Power BI** – Dashboard development and data visualization

**Dataset**

A simulated retail dataset containing Orders, customers, products, and sales data with 65000 records across multiple regions and sales channels. Metrics such as revenue, cost, profit, and discounts

**Key KPIs**

Total Revenue
Total Profit
Profit Margin (%)
Total Orders
Total Customers
Average Order Value (AOV)

**Dashboard Features**

Revenue and profit trends over time
Revenue breakdown by product category, region, and sales channel
Top-performing products and sales representatives
Customer growth analysis
Channel performance comparison
Interactive filters (date, region, category, channel, segment)

**Insights**

New customer acquisition has declined significantly over the past two years, while repeat customer volume has steadily increased. This indicates strong customer satisfaction and retention—customers who purchase once are highly likely to return. However, the declining inflow of new customers presents a long-term growth risk if not addressed.

Electronics Category Dominance: The Electronics category is the primary revenue driver, led by high-value products such as Smartphones, Tablets, and Monitors, which frequently appear in large transactions.

Regional Strength (West & East): The West (San Diego, Seattle, San Francisco) and East (New York, Boston, Philadelphia) regions show the highest transaction frequency and sales volume, outperforming North and South regions.

Channel Performance: While Online sales are strong, Retail and Partner channels also contribute significantly, indicating a well-balanced and diversified sales strategy rather than digital-only dependence.

High-Value Product Concentration: A large share of total revenue is concentrated in a small group of products—primarily Tablets, Smartphones, and Monitors—highlighting opportunities for targeted optimization.

**Business Recommendations**

While retention is strong, the business should prioritize attracting new customers to sustain growth: Invest in targeted digital marketing campaigns (social media, search ads) focused on high-performing products like Electronics. Introduce first-time buyer incentives (discounts, bundles, free shipping)

Increase the dominance of Electronics by introducing bundled offers (e.g., Monitors + Laptops) and “frequently bought together” strategies to increase Average Order Value (AOV).

Double down on marketing efforts in high-performing West and East regions. Launch targeted customer acquisition campaigns in underperforming North regions (e.g., Minneapolis, Cleveland)

Maintain a balanced channel mix by continuing investment across Online, Retail, and Partner channels, while optimizing each channel’s efficiency and customer experience.

Product Strategy Focus: Prioritize inventory, promotions, and pricing strategies around high-performing products while exploring ways to improve sales of underperforming categories.
